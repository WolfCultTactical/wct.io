Women's Self Defense Fundamentals

This woman-led course for women teaches foundational self-defense skills from mindset and communication to hand-to-hand and armed engagements.

Course Overview

This introductory course is designed specifically for women with little to no prior experience in self-defense. It provides a safe, beginner-friendly environment to learn essential skills for personal protection, incorporating empty-hand techniques, short knife usage, and concealed carry fundamentals. The curriculum blends practical training with situational awareness, tailored to the needs of female participants and the tactical standards of Wof Cult Tactical PMC.

Duration

Total Length: 2 Days (16 hours total)

Day 1: 8 hours (Empty-Hand Self-Defense & Short Knife Basics

Day 2: 8 hours (Concealed Carry Fundamentals & Integrated Scenarios

Learning Objectives

1. Build confidence in recognizing and responding to threats

2. Foundational empty-hand self-defense techniques.

3. Introduction to safe and effective use of short knives for self-defense.

4. Basic training in concealed carry firearm handling, safety, and deployment.

5. Develop situational awareness and decision-making under stress.

Course Outline

Day 1: Empty-Hand Self-Defense & Short Knife Basics

Time: 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM (with 1 hour lunch break)

1. Introduction and Mindset (1 hour)

- Welcome and course overview by Wolf Cult Tactical instructors.

- Discussion: The importance of self-defense for women in today's world.

- Core principles: awareness, avoidance, and action.

- Mental preparation: overcoming fear and building a defensive mindset.

2. Situational Awareness & Threat Assessment (1 hour)

- Identifying potential threats in everyday environments (e.g. parking lots, homes, public spaces).

- Body language cues of aggressors.

- De-escalation techniques and verbal assertiveness.

3. Empty-Hand Self-Defense Basics(3 hours)

- Stance and movement: Maintaining balance and mobility.

- Key strikes: Palm strikes, elbow strikes, and knee kicks, targeting vulerable areas (eyes, throat, groin).

- Escapes: Breaking wrist grabs, chokeholds, and bear hugs.

- Practice: Partner drills with padded equipment for realistic simulation.

4. Introduction to Short Knives (2 hours)

- Knife selection: Overview of concealable short knives (e.g. 3-4 inch blades) suitable for women.

- Safety: Handling, carrying, and drawing a knife responsibly.

- Basic techniqus: Thrusts, slashes, and defensive blocks.

- Legal considerations: Brief overview of knife laws (specific to jurisdiction).

- Practice: Controlled drills wit htraining knives (blunt replicas).

5. Day 1 Wrap-Up (1 hour)

- Recap of techniqus and key takeaways.

- Q&A with instructors.

- Homework: Mental rehearsal of awareness and basic movements.

Day 2: Concealed Carry Fundamentals & Integrated Scenarios

Time: 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM (with 1-hour lunch break)

1. Firearm Safety & Concealed Carry Overview (2 hours)

- Four rules of firearm safety

- Introduction to concealed carry

- Choosing a beginner-friendly handgun

- Legal considerations

2. Basic Firearm Handling (2 hours)

- Grip, stance, and sight alignment

- Loading, unloading, and malfunction clearing

- Drawing from concealment: safe and efficient techniques.

- Dry-fire practice: Simulating draws and trigger control with unloaded firearms.

3. Live-Fire Fundamentals (2 hours)

- Range safety briefing.

- Shooting basics: controlled firing at 3-7 yards.

- Emphasis on accuracy and follow-through.

- Practice: 50 rounds per participant (ammunition provided)

4. Integrated Self-Defense Scenarios (1 hour)

- Combining skills: Empty-hand, knife, and firearm responses to simulated threats.

- Scenarios: responding to a grab, a close-range attacker, or a weapon-drawn agressor.

- Stress inoculation: Controlled simulation with instructors.

5. Day 2 Wrap-Up (1 hour)

- Debrief: Lessons learned and self-assessment.

- Certificate of completion awarded by Wolf Cult Tactical PMC.

- Next Steps: Recommendations will be made for intermediate training or practice.

Instructor Qualifications

- Certified Wolf Cult Tactical PMC instructors (female and male) with professional experience in military and law-enforcement hand-to-hand, edged-weapons, and firearms instruction.

Cost

$300 (USD) per person (includes ammo, gear, and certificate)

Discounts available for group and organizational bookings

Men's Self-Defense Fundamentals

Course Overview

This introductory course is designed for men with little to no prior self-defense experience, providing a robust foundation in personal protection. It integrates empty-hand combat, short knife techniques, and concealed carry firearms training, designed to build confidence, physical capability, and tactical awareness in a high-energy, practical setting reflective of Wolf Cult Tactical PMC's standards.

Duration

- Total Length: 2 Days (16 hours total)

- Day 1: 8 hours (Empty-Hand Self-Defense & Short Knife Basics

- Day 2: 8 hours (Concealed Carry Fundamentals & Integrated Scenarios)

Objectives

1. Develop confidence in handling physical threats.

2. Master foundational empty-hand self-defense techniques.

3. Learn safe and effective short knife deployment for self-defense.

4. Gain basic proficiency in concealed carry firearm use and safety.

5. Enhance situational awareness and decision-making under pressure.

Course Outline

Day 1: Empty-Hand Self-Defense & Short Knife Basics

ime: 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM (with 1-hour lunch break)

1. Introduction & Mindset (1 hour)

- Welcome and course overview by Wolf Cult Tactical instructors.

- Discussion: Self-defense priorities for men in modern contexts.

- Core principles: Awareness, aggression, and control.

- Mental preparation: Channeling adrenaline and maintaining focus.

2. Situational Awareness & Threat Assessment (1 hour)

- Reading environments: Identifying risks in urban, rural, or crowded settings.

- Recognizing pre-attack indicators (e.g. posture, group dynamics).

- Verbal confrontation strategies and boundary-setting.

3. Empty-Hand Self-Defense Basics (3 hours)

- Stance and movement: Power generation and stability.

- Key strikes: Punches, elbow strikes, and knee strikes, targeting vital areas (jaw, solar plexus, groin).

- Defense maneuvers: Blocking, redirection and escaping holds (e.g. headlocks, shirt grabs).

- Practice: partner drills with padded gear.

4. Introduction to Short Knives (2 hours)

- Knife selection: Overview of concealable short knives (e.g. 3 - 5 inch blades)

- Safety: Grip, carry methods, and safe drawing techniques.

- Core techniques: Thrusts, cuts, and defensive counters.

- Legal considerations: Brief overview of knife laws (specific to jurisdiction).

- Practice: Dynamic drills with training knives.

5. Day 1 Wrap-Up (1-hour)

- Recap of skills and key lessons.

- Q&A with instructors.

- Homework: visualization of techniques and awareness exercises.

Day 2: Concealed Carry Fundamentals & Integrated Scenarios

Time: 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM (with 1-hour lunch break)

1. Firearm Safety & Concealed Carry Overview

- Four rules of firearm safety.

- Concealed carry for men

- Handgun selection: Beginner-friendly options

- Legal considerations: brief overview of concealed carry laws (specific to jurisdiction)

2. Basic Firearm Handling (2 hours)

- Grip, stance, and recoil managment.

- Loading, unloading, and clearing malfunctions.

- Drawing from concealment: Efficient and controlled techniques.

- Dry-fire practice: Simulating draws and trigger discipline with unloaded firearms.

3. Live-Fire Fundamentals (2 hours)

- Range safety briefing.

- Shooting drills: Controlled firing at 5-10 yards (practical self defense ranges).

- Focus on speed, accuracy, and follow-up shots.

- Practice: 50 rounds per participant (ammunition provided)

4. Integrated Self-Defense Scenarios (1 hour)

- Combining skills: Empty-hand, knife and firearm responses in simulated confrontations.

- Scenarios: Defending against an unarmed attacker, knife-wielding assailant, or multiple threats.

- Stress training: Simulations with instructors.

5. Day 2: Wrap-Up (1 hour)

- Debrief: Reflecting on performance and growth.

- Certificate of completion awarded by Wolf Cult Tactical PMC

- Next Steps: Suggestions for intermedated courses or follow-up practice.

Instructor Qualifications

- Certified Wolf Cult Tactical PMC instructors (female and male) with professional experience in military and law-enforcement hand-to-hand, edged-weapons, and firearms instruction.
